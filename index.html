The Cold-Press Tradition

Our artisan process starts with hand-picked olives, pressed within hours of harvest to ensure zero oxidation. We extract the oil using mechanical means at temperatures below 27°C (80°F).

This meticulous method preserves the delicate polyphenols, giving our olive oil its peppery kick and extraordinary health benefits.

Tasting Etiquette

The 5 S's of Sensory Olive Oil Analysis

Just like fine wine, extra virgin olive oil has complex flavor dimensions. Master the professional tasting technique used by olive oil sommeliers to fully appreciate our reserve collections.

1 See
2 Swirl
3 Smell
4 Slurp
5 Swallow

See the Golden-Green Glow

Pour about one tablespoon of olive oil into a small glass (preferably a blue tasting glass to avoid bias). Look at the color, opacity, and body of the oil.

Sommelier Fact: While color doesn't indicate flavor quality, a vibrant green or gold hue is beautiful and showcases the early-harvest extraction of fresh olives.

Swirl & Warm the Oil

Cup the tasting glass in one hand and cover the top with your other hand. Gently swirl the glass for about 60 seconds.

Sommelier Fact: Warming the oil with your body heat activates the volatile organic compounds in the oil. Keeping it covered traps these aromatic molecules inside the glass, concentrating them for the next step.

Smell the Complex Aromas

Bring the glass directly to your nose, remove your covering hand, and take a deep, slow breath. Notice the immediate aromas.

Sommelier Fact: Premium early-harvest olive oils smell green, fresh, and complex. Look for notes of freshly cut grass, green tomato leaf, green apple, artichoke, herbs, or even green almond.

Slurp to Emulsify

Take a small sip of the oil. Do not swallow yet! Inhale sharply through your mouth with your teeth closed, making a slurping sound.

Sommelier Fact: The sharp intake of air ("strippaggio" in Italian) emulsifies the oil with your saliva, spraying it as a fine mist over your tongue and palate. This allows all your tastebuds to detect bitterness and fruitiness.

Swallow & Feel the Finish

Finally, swallow the oil. Pay attention to the sensations at the back of your throat as you swallow.

Sommelier Fact: A high-quality oil will produce a distinct peppery tickle, scratch, or even a cough in the back of your throat. This is the mark of oleocanthal, a powerful, healthy polyphenol antioxidant.

Our Promise to the Planet

Sustainability is at the heart of our tasting room. From hand-harvesting in organic groves to solar-powering our mills and running a zero-waste operation, we honor the land that feeds us.

100% Organic

Farming in harmony with nature. Our biodynamic farming methods are completely chemical-free, nurturing the soil and the trees naturally.

Zero Waste

Using every single part of the olive. Olive pomace and wastewater are repurposed for organic agricultural compost and heating.

Solar Powered

Renewable energy at our mill. Our pressing facilities run entirely on solar power, resulting in a carbon-negative extraction process.
